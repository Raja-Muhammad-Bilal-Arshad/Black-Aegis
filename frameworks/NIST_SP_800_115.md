# NIST SP 800-115 (Penetration Testing Guide)

> The Federal Standard for Information Security Testing and Assessment.
> **Protocol**: "Process ensures consistency and safety."

## Phase 1: Planning
> "He who fails to plan, plans to fail."
- **Rules of Engagement (ROE)**: Define scope, timing, and emergency contacts.
- **Scope Verification**: Confirm IPs belong to the client.
- **Team Selection**: Assign agents based on `Tier` and `Domain` expertise.

## Phase 2: Discovery
> "Information is ammunition."
- **Passive Recon**: No touching. OSINT, WHOIS, DNS.
- **Active Scanning**: Nmap, Nessus, Burp Suite.
- **Vulnerability Analysis**: Triage findings based on exploitability (Exploit Research Master).

## Phase 3: Attack (Execution)
> "Break in. Stay in."
- **Gaining Access**: Phishing, Exploitation, Weak Creds.
- **Escalating Privileges**: User -> Admin -> System.
- **System Browsing**: Lateral movement to high-value targets.
- **Installing Additional Tools**: Persistence & C2 (Malware Domain Commander).

## Phase 4: Reporting
> "The job isn't done until the paperwork is signed."
- **Analysis**: Map findings to Risk (Business Impact).
- **Remediation**: Provide actionable fix instructions.
- **Deliverables**:
    1.  Executive Summary (Tier 5 Validation Officer).
    2.  Technical Report (Tier 1-4 Operators).
    3.  Raw Data (Logs/Screenshots).
